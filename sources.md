### Slack Spotlight AI Update Filter

Use Slack AI updates as sources, not as raw newsletter copy. Include items only when they have clear UX relevance: design workflow impact, product experience implications, UX research value, design-system or UI engineering relevance, responsible-AI/trust impact, or a hands-on opportunity for designers, researchers, UX writers, or UX engineers.

For the public newsletter site, summarize the selected items and remove internal-only details unless reviewed for publication. For internal-only editions, Slack thread links may be retained after review.

#### Completion gate

Prefer completed or evidence-backed updates over speculative discussion. Treat an item as ready for newsletter consideration when it has at least one of these signals:

- **Shipped or available**: Launched, released, available now, rolled out, enabled, published, recorded, or open for sign-up.
- **Reusable artifact exists**: Playbook, deck, recording, prompt, skill, profile, gem, MCP setup, Figma file, template, dashboard, demo, or source thread.
- **Pilot has learning**: Early users, adoption notes, before/after workflow impact, feedback, evaluation results, or a known limitation.
- **Clear owner or next action**: Team, owner, support channel, install step, workshop date, or concrete follow-up.
- **UX case completed**: A real UX/research/design workflow changed after AI, especially with measurable time saved, quality improvement, or reusable practice.

#### Include signals

Include a Slack AI update when it has at least one completion signal and at least one of these UX-facing signals:

- **Design workflow impact**: AI tools, skills, agents, profiles, prompts, plugins, or setup bundles that help with critique, prototyping, design QA, research synthesis, content generation, or handoff.
- **Product experience relevance**: Updates that affect AI-powered assistants, automation, recommendations, search, support, decisioning, or other user-facing AI experiences.
- **UX research value**: Experiments, evaluations, adoption signals, user feedback, benchmarks, failure cases, or qualitative learnings about how people use or trust AI.
- **Design-system or UI engineering relevance**: AI-assisted component generation, accessibility checks, frontend coding agents, UI testing, BUI/design-system migration, or design-to-code workflows.
- **Responsible AI and trust**: Privacy, data retention, explainability, compliance, hallucination risk, safety controls, model governance, or human-review changes that UX teams should account for.
- **Hands-on opportunities**: Betas, demos, workshops, recordings, templates, playbooks, installable tools, or internal pilots that UX readers can try or learn from.

#### Exclude or down-rank

Exclude or down-rank Slack AI updates that are mostly:

- Backend-only infrastructure changes with no visible UX, workflow, research, or design-system implication.
- Routine version bumps, dependency updates, auth changes, or maintenance notes unless they unlock a UX-relevant capability.
- Internal implementation details that would not help a UX reader make a design, research, product, content, or tooling decision.
- Duplicates of items already covered recently, unless there is a meaningful milestone, outcome, or new lesson.
- Speculative ideas without a demo, owner, evidence, concrete next step, or clear learning value.
- Raw model/version announcements unless they unlock a concrete UX, content, prototyping, image, trust, or product-feasibility capability.

#### Scoring

Score each candidate from 0 to 10:

- **3 pts: UX relevance** — directly affects design, research, product experience, content, prototyping, accessibility, or frontend workflow.
- **2 pts: Actionability** — readers can try, join, reuse, watch, install, discuss, or follow up.
- **2 pts: Evidence** — includes a demo, thread, recording, evaluation result, adoption signal, benchmark, or concrete example.
- **1 pt: Novelty** — introduces a new capability, lesson, decision, or meaningful milestone.
- **1 pt: Local relevance** — comes from China/APAC teams or has clear relevance to local UX work.
- **1 pt: Clarity** — can be explained in 1-2 newsletter sentences without heavy technical setup.

Use these thresholds:

- **8-10**: Include as a highlight-style Slack Spotlight card.
- **5-7**: Include only as a short Slack Spotlight card if the week benefits from the added signal.
- **3-4**: Keep for watchlist or run summary; do not publish by default.
- **0-2**: Exclude.

#### Summary format

For included Slack updates:

- Keep the original Slack permalink in `data-slack-link`.
- Use the real Slack source name in `data-slack-author` and the source channel in `data-slack-channel`.
- Write the visible card as a filtered summary, not raw bot text.
- For every card, keep the visible card description short and scannable with two clear labels: `What is the update: [what changed]` and `Why it's valuable for UXers: [why UXers should care]`.
- In the summary, name the UX implication first, then mention the selected UX-relevant signals.
- Keep the original-message modal concise: cite that it is curated from the internal source and list only the selected UX-relevant signals.
