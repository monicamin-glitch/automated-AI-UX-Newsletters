Claude Design: AI-Powered Prototyping That Challenges Figma

Anthropic launched Claude Design, a tool that lets users create polished prototypes, pitch decks, one-pagers, and marketing collateral through conversational prompts. Powered by Claude Opus 4.7, it generates initial designs you can refine through direct edits or follow-up requests. It integrates with existing design systems and exports to Canva, PDF, PPTX, or HTML — and packages designs for Claude Code handoff. For UX teams, this means rapid prototyping without opening Figma, especially useful for early ideation and stakeholder presentations where speed matters more than pixel precision.

The New Frontier of AI-Native Design Systems

Ken Olewiler argues on UXmatters that design systems must evolve from static Figma libraries into dynamic frameworks that guide AI generation. He identifies six essential capabilities: machine-readable foundations, a reasoning layer capturing design principles, multimodal interaction primitives (for conversational UI), generative assembly, policy-as-code guardrails, and bidirectional learning. For design system teams, this is a strategic roadmap — your system isn't just documentation anymore, it's the instruction set that determines whether AI-generated output meets your quality bar.
